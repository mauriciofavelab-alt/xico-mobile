## Why the editor choice matters

The manifesto names four editors:

| Editor | Mode | Voice |
|---|---|---|
| **María Vázquez** | Institutional gravitas. Cultural authority. Cites figures, recovers history. | Long sentence, declarative, "Casa de México sabe…" |
| **Sofía Niño de Rivera** | Observational humor. Comedian's eye. Reads people, not monuments. | Quick, sharp, "Aquí no se viene a aprender. Se viene a quedarse." |
| **Damián Ortega** | Visual artist's gaze. Materials, objects, juxtaposition. Slow looking. | Sparse, concrete, "La piedra de un molcajete pesa más que el discurso." |
| **Andrés Felipe Solano** | Writer in exile. Memory + displacement. Long-form narrative. | Inflected with absence, "Madrid es una calle a la que México llega tarde." |

Week 19's María opened with Casa de México — institutional weight. Week 20's job is to **shift register**, not double down. Two serious institutional weeks in a row would flatten the editorial promise. The product needs to feel like a magazine with four columnists, not one. Choose the editor whose voice creates the most contrast with last week, while still earning the same standard of taste.

## Option A · Sofía Niño de Rivera (recommended)

**Why:** Tonal counterpoint to María. Same intelligence, different register. Comedians are the best observers in any culture — Sofía sees what an institutional curator doesn't. Her Madrid Ruta would lean toward **night, food, bars with attitude, a salsa club, a 4am taquería**. The despachos write themselves as small character sketches — the people behind the counter, the regulars, the recurring jokes.

**Risk:** Could read as "just bars and food" if not tightly written. María established the gravitas floor; Sofía has to land jokes that don't reduce the editorial standard.

**Why the risk is worth it:** Week 2 is when most "weekly culture apps" pivot to a second Damián-like serious voice and lose 40% of the audience. Sofía retains the readers who walked Week 1 AND brings in readers who would never have touched a Casa de México opener.

## Recommendation

**Ship Option A · Sofía Niño de Rivera.** Here's why this is the right call for Week 20 specifically:

1. **The audience signal.** Week 19 brought in readers who said "yes" to an institutional opener. Week 20 needs to test whether they'll come back for a different voice. If they do — the product is a magazine. If they only respond to serious cultural authority — it's a single-voice newsletter pretending to be a magazine.
2. **The Madrid context.** It's late May. Spring nights. The city is outdoors after 11 PM. A "Madrid de medianoche" Ruta written by a comedian lines up with what's actually happening on the street that week — Week 19's institutional spaces are tied to opening hours, Week 20's bars and 4am taquerías match the season.
3. **Editor availability (assumed).** Sofía is a public figure with a live performance schedule; getting 5 short despachos from her requires email outreach and a clear ask. Damián's the same risk; Andrés is in Seoul. María was already pre-aligned via the seed. The realistic path is "Sofía sends 5 short voice notes, you transcribe" — feasible for week 20.
4. **The risk is bounded.** If Sofía's despachos read as too light, you reframe the next Ruta with a heavier hand. Week 20 is recoverable. The unrecoverable mistake is two consecutive María-shaped weeks.
